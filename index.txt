<div class = "item myinfo" id = "tribute-info"> <i>My Bloody Valentine</i> are an alternative rock band that was formed in Dublin in 1983. They are perhaps the most famous and popular shoegaze band - shoegaze is a sub-genre of alternative rock characterised by the use of noisy feedback and distortion to create a wall of sound. Since 1987, the band has been made up by Kevin Shields (vocalist, guitarist and sampler), Colm Ó Cíosóig (drummer and sampler), Bilinda Butcher (vocalist and guitarist) and Debbie Googe (bassist).<br></br>The band got its name in 1983 at the suggestion of David Conway, an early member who was once the lead vocalist. (Shields has since claimed to have been unaware of the 1981 slasher film of the same name at the time.)<br></br></div>

<div class = "item myinfo" id = "myinfo2"> <h1>Early Period: <i>EPs, Isn't Anything</i></h1> <i>My Bloody Valentine</i> put out their first release in <i>This is Your Bloody Valentine</i> in 1985 but it didn’t receive much attention. Their second EP <i>The New Record by My Bloody Valentine</i> which was recorded and released in 1986. It was a minor success, reaching 22 on the UK Indie Chart, allowing the band to perform more frequently and begin developing a small following. After <i>My Bloody Valentine</i> opened for his band <i>Biff Bang Pow!</i> in January 1988, Alan McGee approached the band and invited them to record and release a single on Creation Records, of which he was a co-founder. They released <i>You Made Me Realise</i> later that year which was positively received by independent music critics and debuted at number 2 on the UK Indie Chart. 
  <br></br>
Just a few months later they would release their first full-length album, <i>Isn’t Anything</i>. It was the band’s first major success, receiving the highest critical acclaim so far and peaking at number 1 on the UK Indie Chart, as well as having a great influence on other shoegaze bands of the time. </div>

<div class = "item myinfo" id = "myinfo3"> <h1>Loveless</h1> Recording for the band’s second album was set to begin in February 1989, with Creation Records expecting the album to be completed in around five days. This would prove to be a comically vast underestimate. After years of stop-start recording marred by constant delays, studio changes and health issues (both Shields and Butcher were afflicted with tinnitus; Creation’s second-in-command suffered a nervous breakdown), the album was finally completed in 1991. 
<br></br>
Most of the music on <i>Loveless</i> was written and performed by Shields with vocal and lyrical contributions from Butcher. Despite its near-unanimous critical acclaim, the album was not a commercial success and after the extensive and expensive recording period, as well as his interpseronal issues with Shields, McGee dropped <i>My Bloody Valentine</i> from Creation Records. The band fell apart not long after, recording and/or performing together only rarely for the following two decades. Shields explained that his inability to deliver a follow-up was due to a lack of inspiration in an interview with Magnet magazine. The album was hugely influential on a number of artists such as Robert Smith of <i>The Cure</i> and Billy Corgan of <i>The Smashing Pumpkins</i>. It is regarded by many to be one of the best albums of the 90s and of all time. </div>

    <div class = "item myinfo" id = "myinfo4"> Shields was inspired by both 60s pop and hip hop when making <i>Loveless</i>, both in terms of sound and of technique. In <i>Loveless</i>’ dense textural soundscapes one can hear a call-back the Wall of Sound, developed and utilised by Phil Spector as well as similar recording techniques which were used by Brian Wilson at the time. Then there’s the influence of Public Enemy and the Bomb Squad, most notably their use of sampling. By early 1991 the band had acquired an Akai sampler. Other equipment used by Shields included Fender Jaguars and Jazzmasters, which he altered by extending the tremolo arm and loosening it so that he was able to manipulate it while strumming. He also used reverb from units such as the Alesis Midiverb II and the Yamaha SPX90.  </div>

<div class = "item" id = "myinfo5">  </div>

<div class = "item myinfo" id = "myinfo6"> In the years following <i>Loveless</i>' release, Shields teased a third release regularly but nothing materilised. He received comparisons to musicians such as Brian Wilson and Syd Barrett in the music press due to his reputation as a recluse and his inability to deliver a follow-up. He explained his lack of musical output during this time as being due to a lack of inspiration in an interview with Magnet magazine. Finally, the last of the recording, mixing and mastering was completed in 2012. 
</div>

<div class = "item myinfo" id = "myinfo7"><h1>mbv</h1> In 2013, the album <i>mbv</i> was finally released. The band chose to put it out on their own label, mbv Records. It was made available to purchase on their website, which crashed within minutes of the album's release due to heavy web traffic. Though the reception to  <i>Loveless</i> made for a tough follow-up, this album too was released to critical acclaim. It contained many elements familiar to <i>My Bloody Valentine</i >'s ouevre such as the lush soundscapes, the buried and adrongynous vocals and the unconventional song structures but with some new directions too, paerticularly in its final three tracks which Alexis Petridis described as "unlike anything My Bloody Valentine have released before" in his review for the Guardian. </div>
